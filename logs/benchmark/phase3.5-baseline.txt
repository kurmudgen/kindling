==========================================================================
KEY FINDING: LEARNED WEIGHTS CHANGED SYSTEM BEHAVIOR
==========================================================================

The Phase 3 sleep analyst diagnosed that Phase 2/3 was OVER-ESCALATING
on low-valence queries — escalating even when composite valence was
under 0.25 and urgency/stakes were zero. It recommended:
  - escalationThreshold: 0.28 → 0.36 (+0.08)
  - tokenProbabilitySpreadWeight: 0.35 → 0.24 (-0.11)
  - semanticVelocityWeight: 0.25 → 0.31 (+0.06)
  - attentionAnomalyWeight: 0.15 → 0.20 (+0.05)
  - deescalationThreshold: 0.15 → 0.18 (+0.03)

Phase 3.5 loaded these learned weights (age=0, full effect) via the
decay-aware config merge. The behavior changed as predicted:

1. ZERO post-start escalations. The higher threshold means signals
   from the 1.5b model no longer cross the escalation line for
   moderately-complex queries.

2. More queries stay at Tier 1 (44 vs 29 in Phase 3). The learned
   weights are more tolerant of moderate spread/velocity signals.

3. The remaining Tier 2/3 queries (11 total) all started there via
   the valence gate — not through signal-based escalation. These are
   queries with composite > 0.35 (Tier 2 gate) or composite > 0.50
   + stakes > 0.5 (Tier 3 gate).

4. HUGE latency wins on medium queries: 53.4s → 22.2s (-58%). Medium
   queries that Phase 3 incorrectly escalated to the slow 14b model
   now run entirely on the fast 1.5b model.

5. Hard queries also faster: 201s → 161s (-20%). Fewer unnecessary
   Tier 3 (32b) calls.

==========================================================================
WAS THIS THE RIGHT CALL?
==========================================================================

The learned weights made the system more aggressive about staying at
Tier 1. Two ways to read this:

OPTIMISTIC: The sleep analyst correctly identified false escalations
and fixed them. Quality stayed the same (all 55 pass, coherence >0.8
across all categories) while cutting medium-query latency by 58%.
This is the adaptive learning working as designed.

SKEPTICAL: The new weights may be OVER-conservative. Medium queries
that legitimately benefit from the 14b model's better output are now
served by the 1.5b model. Without a human quality review, we can't
tell if the responses are as good or just "good enough".

The correct answer depends on per-query quality evaluation, which
Phase 4's shadow mode (compare against Claude responses) will enable.
For now, the system is doing exactly what it was told to do by its
own learned weights — and the analyst's diagnosis was specifically
targeted at the low-valence escalation class that was the dominant
pattern in Phase 2/3 logs.
